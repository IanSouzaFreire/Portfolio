Political theory is the study of ideas and concepts related to politics, governance, and justice. It examines the nature of political institutions, the role of citizens, and the principles behind political decisions and ideologies. Political theory explores foundational questions about authority, power, rights, and the ideal structure of society. The origins of political theory date back to ancient civilizations. Early thinkers like Plato and Aristotle in Greece laid the groundwork with their discussions on democracy, ethics, and political philosophy. Plato's “Republic” and Aristotle's “Politics” are seminal works that continue to influence political thought.
